Our Mission

We don't just grow food.
We grow communities, soil, and futures.

Four corporations control more than 80% of the meat we eat. A handful of seed giants set the price of every row. Trade deals reward scale over stewardship. Meanwhile, the people doing the actual work — small and mid-sized farmers — are squeezed harder every season.

Farmers Fightback exists to change that. We're building a durable coalition that puts growers, workers, and eaters on the same side of the table. Because food is too important to leave to monopolies.

  • Fair prices for the people who grow your food
  • Antitrust enforcement against ag monopolies
  • Right to repair, right to seed, right to organize
  • A climate-resilient, regenerative food system

"They told us to get big or get out. We chose a third option: fight back, together."

DM Dolores Marquez Third-generation rancher, New Mexico
What We Fight For

Five fronts. One movement.

These are the fights shaping the future of who grows food, who eats well, and who profits. We're in every one of them.

Break Up The Cartels

Four meatpackers. Two seed monopolies. One grain trader for half the world. We're pushing antitrust action that actually bites.

Fair Prices, Honest Markets

Cost-of-production pricing, transparent contracts, and an end to the captive supply schemes that crush independent producers.

Right To Repair & Seed

You should be able to fix your own tractor and save your own seed. We're winning that right back, statehouse by statehouse.

Soil & Climate Resilience

Funding regenerative practices, cover crops, and water stewardship — because soil that's alive is the only insurance that matters.

Workers' Rights On The Farm

The people who pick, pack, and process deserve safe conditions, fair wages, and the right to organize without retaliation.

Healthy Food For Every Zip Code

Connecting independent farms to schools, hospitals, and neighborhoods that big distributors have written off.

Voices

Stories from the fight.

Iowa

"They wrote me a contract I couldn't refuse — until we read it together."

When a regional poultry integrator tried to lock 40 growers into one-sided contracts, the local Fightback chapter brought a labor lawyer to the kitchen table. Six months later, the contracts were public, and three clauses were dead.

— Sam & Renee Holcomb, contract growers
California

"We built a co-op packing house, and the prices stopped lying."

Twenty-two specialty crop farms pooled resources to open a member-owned packing facility. Within a season, they cut middlemen by half and raised member returns 19%.

— Valley Roots Cooperative
Georgia

"Right to repair was a kitchen-table issue. Now it's law."

After a record harvest sat in fields waiting on a $12,000 service call, our members organized testimony from 80 farmers. The state passed agricultural right-to-repair the next session.

— Coastal Plains chapter
